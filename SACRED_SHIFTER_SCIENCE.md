## 1. Coherence (Coh)

### Scientific Basis

**Primary Phenomenon:** Heart-breath synchronization, autonomic nervous system phase-locking

**Key Research Areas:**
- **Respiratory Sinus Arrhythmia (RSA)**: Heart rate naturally increases during inhalation and decreases during exhalation. High-amplitude RSA indicates healthy parasympathetic tone.
- **Heart Rate Variability (HRV) Coherence**: HeartMath Institute's coherence metric measures ordered, sine-wave-like oscillations in heart rate at ~0.1 Hz (breathing frequency).
- **Neurocardiac Synchrony**: Coherent heart rhythms correlate with prefrontal cortex activation and improved emotional regulation.
- **Oscillatory Entrainment**: When multiple biological oscillators (heart, breath, brain waves) phase-lock, cognitive and emotional stability increases.

**Key Papers:**
- McCraty, R., & Childre, D. (2010). "Coherence: Bridging Personal, Social, and Global Health." *Alternative Therapies in Health and Medicine*, 16(4), 10-24.
- Lehrer, P. M., & Gevirtz, R. (2014). "Heart rate variability biofeedback: how and why does it work?" *Frontiers in Psychology*, 5, 756.
- Porges, S. W. (2011). *The Polyvagal Theory: Neurophysiological Foundations of Emotions*. Norton.

**Measurement:**
- **Gold Standard**: ECG-derived HRV with spectral analysis
- **Proxy Measures**: Breath regularity, galvanic skin response stability, interaction rhythm consistency

**Interpretation:**
- **High Coherence (0.7-1.0)**: Strong parasympathetic tone, emotional regulation, calm alertness
- **Moderate Coherence (0.4-0.7)**: Functional state, some stress resilience
- **Low Coherence (0-0.4)**: Sympathetic dominance, emotional dysregulation, fragmented attention

## 3. Polarity Balance (Pol)

### Scientific Basis

**Primary Phenomenon:** Balance between opposing forces (sympathetic/parasympathetic, positive/negative affect, left/right hemispheres)

**Key Research Areas:**
- **Opponent-Process Theory**: Solomon & Corbit (1974) - every emotional state triggers an opposing process. Balance = emotional stability.
- **Hemispheric Asymmetry**: Left hemisphere (approach, positive affect) vs. right hemisphere (withdrawal, negative affect). Balance predicts well-being.
- **Autonomic Balance**: Sympathetic (fight/flight) vs. Parasympathetic (rest/digest). Measured via LF/HF ratio in HRV.
- **Emotional Valence**: Positive vs. negative affect balance. Russell's circumplex model.

**Key Papers:**
- Solomon, R. L., & Corbit, J. D. (1974). "An opponent-process theory of motivation." *Psychological Review*, 81(2), 119-145.
- Davidson, R. J. (2004). "What does the prefrontal cortex 'do' in affect?" *Perspectives on Psychological Science*, 1(3), 219-234.
- Thayer, J. F., & Lane, R. D. (2009). "Claude Bernard and the heartâ€“brain connection: Further elaboration of a model of neurovisceral integration." *Neuroscience & Biobehavioral Reviews*, 33(2), 81-88.

**Measurement:**
- **Gold Standard**: EEG frontal asymmetry, HRV LF/HF ratio, affect balance questionnaires
- **Proxy Measures**: Choice polarity (left/right, yes/no balance), color temperature preference, spatial bias

**Interpretation:**
- **Balanced Polarity (0.45-0.55)**: Emotional equanimity, integrated opposites, "clear but not suppressed"
- **Extreme Polarity (0-0.2 or 0.8-1.0)**: Unidirectional dominance, reactivity, emotional suppression or overwhelm

## 4. Uncertainty (U)

### Scientific Basis

**Primary Phenomenon:** Bayesian prediction error, cognitive dissonance, model updating

**Key Research Areas:**
- **Predictive Processing / Free Energy Principle**: Karl Friston's framework - the brain constantly predicts sensory input and updates when predictions fail.
- **Prediction Error Coding**: Anterior cingulate cortex (ACC) tracks mismatch between expected and actual outcomes.
- **Cognitive Dissonance**: Festinger (1957) - uncertainty and conflict drive model updating.
- **Entropy in Bayesian Inference**: High uncertainty = high entropy = many possible states.

**Key Papers:**
- Friston, K. (2010). "The free-energy principle: a unified brain theory?" *Nature Reviews Neuroscience*, 11(2), 127-138.
- Botvinick, M. M., Cohen, J. D., & Carter, C. S. (2004). "Conflict monitoring and anterior cingulate cortex: an update." *Trends in Cognitive Sciences*, 8(12), 539-546.
- Festinger, L. (1957). *A Theory of Cognitive Dissonance*. Stanford University Press.

**Measurement:**
- **Gold Standard**: fMRI of ACC activation, EEG error-related negativity (ERN), pupil dilation
- **Proxy Measures**: Decision hesitancy, exploration rate, response time variance

**Interpretation:**
- **High Uncertainty (0.7-1.0)**: Openness, exploration, vulnerability, "not knowing"
- **Moderate Uncertainty (0.4-0.7)**: Healthy learning, adaptive updating
- **Low Uncertainty (0-0.4)**: Confidence, rigidity, closed models
